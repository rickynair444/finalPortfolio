Rick Owen's Iconic Collections

Spring/Summer 2014: Vicious

The Vicious collection was one of Rick Owens' most famous runway show. This show has aggressive, warrior silhouettes. This collection spurred cotroversy among society as it pushed the boundaries of modern masculinity and fashion's expression.

Fall/Winter 2018: Sisyphus

The Sisyphus Collection was inspired by the Greek myth of Sisyphus, was a collection about struggle, repetition, and resistance. The collection featured dramatic, sculptural garments with exaggerated silhouettes, draped fabrics, and experimental shoes, embodying Sisyphus' endless, uphill battle of pushing a boulder up the mountain. Owens blended elements of dark, apocalyptic imagery with high fashion, projecting a double impression of strength and fatigue, which symbolizes the eternal struggle of the individual against the forces of society. The collection was a study of the perpetual quest for meaning in the face of inevitable defeat while projecting an image of untamed strength and beauty.

Spring/Summer 2021: Phlegethon

This collection titled Phlegethon was inspired by the mythological river of fire in the underworld, representing purification, torment, and rebirth. The collection showcased striking, bold designs with textures reminiscent of molten metal, featuring asymmetrical cuts and fluid, dramatic silhouettes. Owens infused a dark, apocalyptic vibe, merging futuristic elements with ancient influences. The use of burnt and charred fabrics highlighted themes of destruction and transformation, while the intense color palette of reds, oranges, and blacks underscored the collection’s ties to the underworld and its fiery nature. The collection conveyed a sense of both vulnerability and strength, delving into the idea of emerging more resilient from adversity.
